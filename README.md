🌍 ESG Climate Risk in ASEAN Financial Institutions
A Quantitative Assessment of Physical and Transition Risk in Malaysian Banks

Author: Arif Shaikhan
Programme: Actuarial Science, UKM
Type: Independent Research Project (2026)

📌 Overview

This project quantifies climate-related financial risk for Malaysia’s three largest banks — Maybank, CIMB, and Public Bank — using NGFS climate scenarios, BNM CRST 2024 framework, and public financial disclosures.

It translates climate risk into monetary impact on bank loan portfolios, covering both:
🌊 Physical risk (flood-exposed lending)
🔥 Transition risk (carbon pricing impact on high-emission sectors)
📉 Portfolio stress testing under NGFS scenarios

🎯 Key Findings
🌊 RM168 billion in estimated flood-exposed loans (~11–12% of portfolios)
🔥 RM8.5 billion+ potential credit losses under RM150/t carbon price scenario
📉 Portfolio losses range from 2.8% to 6.7% under NGFS climate pathways
⚠️ Physical risk is concentrated in household mortgages (55–70% of lending books)
⚠️ Transition risk is driven mainly by manufacturing and energy sectors

🧠 Key Insight
- Malaysian banks face material but largely undisclosed climate risk, with exposure concentrated in property lending and carbon-intensive sectors.
- Early policy action significantly reduces long-term financial losses.

📈 Methodology
This study applies:
- Physical Risk Model : Loans × Sector Exposure × Flood Probability
- Transition Risk Model : High-Carbon Loans × Carbon Price × Sensitivity Factor
- NGFS Stress Testing Framework : Scenario-based portfolio loss estimation across climate pathways
